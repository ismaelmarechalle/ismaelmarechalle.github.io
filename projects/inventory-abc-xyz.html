Inventory ABC-XYZ Classification

Python notebook applying dual ABC (revenue contribution) and XYZ (demand variability) classification to a 500-SKU synthetic dataset, producing a segmentation matrix with operational recommendations.

📓 Deliverable: Jupyter notebook + CSV output
📁 Dataset: 500 SKUs × 12 months demand history
🐍 Libraries: pandas, matplotlib, numpy

The Problem

Not all inventory deserves the same management attention. A company that treats a fast-moving, high-value component the same way as a slow-moving, low-value spare part will over-invest in safety stock for some SKUs and stockout on others. ABC-XYZ classification gives supply chain teams a data-driven way to segment their catalog and apply differentiated policies.

The Method

ABC classification ranks SKUs by annual revenue contribution:

  • A — top 80% of revenue (typically ~20% of SKUs)
  • B — next 15% of revenue
  • C — bottom 5% of revenue (typically ~50% of SKUs)

XYZ classification ranks SKUs by demand variability (coefficient of variation across 12 months):

  • X — CV < 0.5 — stable, predictable demand
  • Y — CV 0.5–1.0 — moderate variability
  • Z — CV > 1.0 — highly variable or intermittent demand

Combining both axes produces a 3×3 segmentation matrix (AX through CZ) that tells you both how important a SKU is and how hard it is to forecast.

Code Walkthrough

# 1. Generate synthetic dataset (reproducible) import pandas as pd import numpy as np import matplotlib.pyplot as plt np.random.seed(42) n_skus = 500 months = [f"M{i:02d}" for i in range(1, 13)] # Simulate monthly demand with varying volatility per SKU demand = np.random.negative_binomial( n=np.random.uniform(2, 20, n_skus).reshape(-1,1), p=0.4, size=(n_skus, 12) ) # 2. ABC: rank by total annual revenue contribution df['annual_revenue'] = df[months].sum(axis=1) * df['unit_price'] df = df.sort_values('annual_revenue', ascending=False) df['cumulative_pct'] = df['annual_revenue'].cumsum() / df['annual_revenue'].sum() df['ABC'] = pd.cut(df['cumulative_pct'], bins=[0, 0.8, 0.95, 1.0], labels=['A','B','C']) # 3. XYZ: coefficient of variation across 12 months df['cv'] = df[months].std(axis=1) / df[months].mean(axis=1) df['XYZ'] = pd.cut(df['cv'], bins=[0, 0.5, 1.0, np.inf], labels=['X','Y','Z']) # 4. Combined segment df['segment'] = df['ABC'].astype(str) + df['XYZ'].astype(str)

Output: Segmentation Matrix

AX
High value, stable demand → prioritize stock availability
AZ
High value, volatile demand → safety stock + close monitoring
CX
Low value, stable demand → simple replenishment rule
CZ
Low value, volatile → consider make-to-order or drop

The notebook outputs a heatmap of SKU count by segment, a bar chart of revenue by ABC class, and a CSV with all SKUs labeled — ready for import into an ERP or WMS.

Operational Recommendations per Segment

  • AX: Continuous review, tight reorder point, supplier partnership for guaranteed availability
  • AY: Periodic review with safety stock buffer; forecast review every quarter
  • AZ: High safety stock or consignment stock; explore demand stabilization with the commercial team
  • BX/BY: Standard periodic review; automate replenishment
  • CZ: Evaluate deletion from catalog or switch to on-demand procurement

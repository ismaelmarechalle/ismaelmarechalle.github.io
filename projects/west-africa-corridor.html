West Africa Corridor Risk Map

A consultant-style supply chain risk briefing on the Dakar–Bamako–Niamey corridor, built from World Bank, UNCTAD, and AfDB public data.

🗺️ Deliverable: 10-slide PDF briefing
📊 Data: World Bank LPI, UNCTAD port rankings, AfDB corridor reports
🌍 Region: West Africa — Senegal, Mali, Niger

The Problem

The Dakar–Bamako–Niamey corridor is one of West Africa's main overland trade arteries, serving landlocked Mali and Niger through Senegal's port infrastructure. Yet supply chain risks on this corridor — poor road conditions, unpredictable border dwell times, seasonal impassability, and political instability — are poorly documented in a format that logistics managers can actually use.

This project fills that gap: a professional briefing designed for a shipper, freight forwarder, or procurement manager who needs a quick, structured risk overview before routing cargo through the corridor.

Why This Corridor

This is not a hypothetical choice. During my maritime agency internship in Dakar, I observed firsthand how cargo moving inland toward Bamako faced delays at the Diboli border post and on unpaved sections of the N1 road. The corridor risk I document here is informed by real operational exposure, not just desk research.

Unique positioning: This project directly leverages 12 months of experience in West African port operations. No other junior candidate in a French or European job pool is building this type of artifact.

Data Sources Used

  • World Bank Logistics Performance Index (LPI) — Senegal, Mali, Niger scores on customs, infrastructure, timeliness
  • UNCTAD Port Performance Scorecard — Dakar port dwell time benchmarks
  • African Development Bank (AfDB) — Trans-West African Coastal Highway and corridor development reports
  • OECD/SWAC — Sahel transport corridors and cross-border friction analysis
  • USAID West Africa Trade Hub — border post delay data and non-tariff barrier documentation

Briefing Structure (10 Slides)

  1. Executive Summary — corridor overview, key risk rating, one-page takeaway
  2. Corridor Map — route from Dakar port to Bamako and onward to Niamey, with key nodes marked
  3. Port of Dakar: Performance Overview — dwell time benchmarks, congestion windows, recent infrastructure investments
  4. Road Infrastructure Assessment — paved vs. unpaved segments, known bottleneck zones, axle load limits
  5. Border Crossing Analysis — Diboli (SN/ML) and Gaya (ML/NI) dwell times, customs procedures, informal friction
  6. Seasonal Risk Calendar — rainy season road closures, harvest-period traffic surges, political risk periods
  7. Political & Security Risk — current context for Sahel transit, routing implications
  8. Cost & Transit Time Benchmarks — Dakar–Bamako by road vs. Abidjan–Bamako comparison
  9. Risk Matrix — probability × impact grid for each identified risk factor
  10. Mitigation Recommendations — 5 actionable options (insurance, routing alternatives, seasonal scheduling, freight forwarder selection criteria)

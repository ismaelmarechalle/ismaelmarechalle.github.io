ERP Process Mapping: Order-to-Cash

Detailed O2C process map with ERP module touchpoints, swimlane diagram, and a 2-page analysis identifying the 3 most common failure points in ERP-managed order flows.

🔄 Deliverable: draw.io diagram + PDF analysis
📋 Process: Order-to-Cash (O2C) end-to-end
💻 ERP scope: Sales, Inventory, Finance modules

The Problem

Many candidates list "ERP experience" on their CV but cannot explain what actually happens inside the system when an order is placed. Recruiters and hiring managers — especially in supply chain coordination, order management, and logistics roles — want to see that you understand the data flows, not just that you clicked through screens.

This project documents the Order-to-Cash process end-to-end: every step, every ERP module involved, every decision point, and the three places where things most commonly go wrong.

Why O2C: Order-to-Cash is the most universal supply chain process — it exists in every company that sells something. Understanding it deeply signals readiness for order management, logistics coordination, and supply chain analyst roles across industries.

Process Steps Mapped

  1. Customer Order Receipt
    Order enters via EDI, email, or web portal → ERP creates a Sales Order (SO). Module: Sales / Order Management.
  2. Credit Check
    ERP checks customer credit limit against outstanding balance. If exceeded: order hold → manual approval required. Module: Finance / Accounts Receivable.
  3. Inventory Availability Check
    ATP (Available-to-Promise) logic checks on-hand stock, open POs, and existing commitments. Module: Inventory Management.
  4. Inventory Reservation
    Quantity reserved for this SO — reduces available inventory for other orders. Triggers replenishment signal if stock falls below reorder point.
  5. Warehouse Pick, Pack & Ship
    WMS generates pick list. Warehouse team picks, packs, generates shipping label and packing list. ERP records goods issue → inventory decremented. Module: WMS / Warehouse Management.
  6. Transport & Delivery
    Carrier picks up. Tracking number linked to SO. ERP may integrate with TMS for real-time visibility. Module: TMS / Logistics.
  7. Customer Receipt Confirmation
    Proof of delivery (POD) received. ERP records delivery confirmation. Any discrepancies (short delivery, damage) trigger claims process.
  8. Invoice Generation
    ERP auto-generates invoice based on SO and goods issue data. Invoice sent to customer (EDI or email). Module: Finance / Billing.
  9. Cash Receipt & Reconciliation
    Payment received → matched against open invoice in ERP. SO closed. Revenue recognized. Module: Finance / AR.

The 3 Most Common Failure Points

① ATP Inaccuracy — Phantom Inventory

ERP shows stock available but physical warehouse is empty (due to unreported damage, theft, or counting error). O2C proceeds to pick stage, then fails — causing emergency expediting or customer delay.

ERP fix: Cycle counting integration — regular partial counts feed the ERP in real time. Variance threshold triggers automatic inventory hold pending recount.

② Credit Hold Without Visibility

Order silently placed on credit hold with no notification to sales, customer service, or the customer. Days pass before anyone notices. Customer relationship damaged; revenue delayed.

ERP fix: Automated alert workflow — credit hold triggers instant email to sales rep + customer service manager + customer (configurable). SLA timer starts at hold creation.

③ Invoice / Delivery Mismatch

Invoice is generated for the full ordered quantity but the shipment was short (partial delivery). Customer disputes invoice; payment is delayed pending resolution. Finance closes month with inflated AR.

ERP fix: Invoice auto-generated from goods issue record, not from SO quantity. Any partial delivery automatically creates a partial invoice. Residual quantity stays open on the SO.

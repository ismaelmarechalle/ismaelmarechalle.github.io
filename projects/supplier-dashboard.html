Supplier Lead Time Dashboard

Power BI report tracking on-time delivery performance, lead time variance, and underperforming suppliers across 12 months of purchase order data.

📊 Deliverable: Power BI report (PDF export)
📁 Dataset: 420 simulated purchase orders
🏭 Scope: 15 suppliers, 5 product categories

The Problem

Procurement teams often track supplier performance in scattered spreadsheets, making it hard to spot trends or compare suppliers objectively. When a supplier starts slipping — longer lead times, more late deliveries — the problem is usually visible only in hindsight, after it has already caused a stockout or a production delay.

This dashboard gives procurement managers a single, interactive view of supplier reliability over time, with enough granularity to act early.

Dataset

The Excel data source contains 420 purchase orders spanning 12 months with the following fields:

  • PO number, PO date
  • Supplier name & ID (15 suppliers)
  • Product category — raw materials, packaging, spare parts, consumables, equipment
  • Country of origin — France, Germany, China, Turkey, Morocco, Senegal
  • Promised delivery date — as committed by supplier at PO confirmation
  • Actual delivery date — recorded at goods receipt
  • PO value (€)

Derived columns calculated in Power BI: lead time (days), delay (days), on-time flag (yes/no), late category (on time / <3 days / 3–7 days / >7 days).

Dashboard Pages

  1. Overview — overall OTD rate, average lead time, total POs, % late by delay band. Slicers: month range, product category, country of origin.
  2. Supplier Scorecard — ranked bar chart of OTD rate by supplier. Conditional formatting flags suppliers below 85% threshold in red. Drill-through to individual supplier detail.
  3. Lead Time Trends — line chart showing monthly average lead time per supplier. Reveals seasonal patterns and deteriorating suppliers before OTD rate drops.
  4. Delay Distribution — histogram of delivery delays in days. Shows whether lateness is clustered (systemic issue) or scattered (random variation).
  5. Top Underperformers — table of the 5 suppliers with highest average delay in the selected period, with PO count and total spend for context.

Power BI Features Used

  • DAX measures: OTD rate, average lead time, delay band classification
  • Slicers and cross-filtering between visuals
  • Conditional formatting on matrix and bar charts
  • Drill-through pages for supplier-level detail
  • Bookmarks for toggling between "All suppliers" and "Underperformers only" views
  • Published to Power BI Service (free account) for shareable URL

Business Value

The dashboard answers the three questions a procurement manager asks every Monday morning:

  • Which suppliers are underperforming right now?
  • Is the problem getting better or worse over time?
  • Which product categories are most exposed to supply risk?
